Australian Animals

Kangaroo | Koala | Wombat | Tasmanian Devil | Platypus

Australia is famous for its unique and diverse wildlife. The continent is home to a wide variety of animals that are found nowhere else in the world. Some of the most famous Australian animals include:

Kangaroo - The Symbol of Australia

Marsupial

Why is the kangaroo so important to Australia? It is a well-known fact that Kangaroos are the symbol of Australia. They are presented in the Australian coat of arms. The first image, which springs into someone’s mind when he is thinking about Australia, is one of a cute Kangaroo. They have been featured on coins, too.

Koala

Marsupial

Koalas are cute, tree-dwelling marsupials native to Australia. They have fluffy gray fur and large, round faces. Koalas primarily eat eucalyptus leaves, which make up their entire diet. They are known for their slow movements and sleep a lot, sometimes up to 20 hours a day. Koalas have strong claws for gripping tree branches, and they rarely come down to the ground. They carry and nurse their tiny, underdeveloped babies in pouches. Koalas are iconic Australian animals and are protected due to habitat loss and disease threats.

Wombat

Marsupial

Wombats are sturdy, short-legged animals from Australia. They eat plants, dig burrows, and come out at night. They carry their babies in pouches, and there are different kinds of wombats. They're protected to preserve Australian wildlife.

Tasmanian Devil

Marsupial

The Tasmanian devil is a small, black carnivorous marsupial found in Tasmania, Australia. It's known for its strong jaws, fierce temperament, and distinctive screeching sounds. These nocturnal animals eat carrion (dead animals) and are currently endangered due to a contagious facial cancer called Devil Facial Tumor Disease. They have unique reproductive methods, and conservation efforts are in place to protect them.

Platypus

Mammalian Monotreme

The platypus is a peculiar Australian animal with a duck-like bill, webbed feet, and lays eggs. It's a great swimmer, primarily eats aquatic creatures, and the males have a venomous spur. Efforts are in place to protect their habitats and ensure their survival.
